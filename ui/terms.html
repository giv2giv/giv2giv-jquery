Schedule A: Policies and Procedures Governing the giv2giv Donor Advised Fund

giv2giv Incorporated

Definitions:

giv2giv.org fund: The Donor Advised Fund operated by giv2giv.org Incorporated.

Board: The Board of Directors of giv2giv Incorporated

Section 1. Establishment and Purpose

1.1 giv2giv.org. (hereinafter “Foundation”) has authorized and ratified the establishment of Advised Funds by Resolution of the Board of Directors (hereinafter “Board”) adopted June 13, 2012. This Resolution authorizes the adoption of these procedures for the administration of Advised Funds. These procedures may be amended from time to time, when deemed necessary or desirable by the Board.

1.2 Function. Foundation software allows donors to create and contribute to mini-endowments. These endowments consist of:

  • A name (e.g. “Cause for Virginia Homeless”)
  • 1-N of a pre-approved investment option (e.g. “Pax World Balanced - PAXWX”)
  • 1-N charities to which the donor advises the endowment to make periodic grants

Foundation will accept and process donations from donors, invest the donations into a pre-approved investment option, and then periodically grant to endowment member charities.

Section 2. Donations, Distributions, Restrictions and Conditions

2.1 Identification. Donations are separately identified by reference to the original donors.

2.2 Value. There is a minimum amount of $5.00 to establish an Advised Fund within the Foundation. The Foundation requests that the Donor consider making additional contributions to the Fund until it reaches at least the $100 level.

The Foundation will not certify to a Donor the value of a contribution of any property or equipment.

2.3 Restrictions and Conditions. Donors may not impose restrictions or conditions on any assets in the Foundation.

2.4 Distributions. A Foundation grants committee will periodically review grant advice made by donors. Should the grants committee concur with the advice given by the Donor, the Foundation will distribute grants to charities after approval of the grant recipients and amounts by the grants committee.

2.5 Structure of Grant Committee. The grant committee will initially consist of three members of the Board of the Foundation. Grant committee structure can be changed by a majority vote of the Board of the Foundation.

2.6 Decision-making authority. The final decision-making authority on how distributions from a donor’s account are made lies with the Foundation.

Section 3. Investment of Fund Assets

3.1 Responsibility. The Foundation has the responsibility and authority for the investment of the assets of each Advised Fund. The assets of any Fund may be commingled with those of other Advised Funds, or with other funds of the Foundation, or may be invested in units of a common investment fund, which may be established or utilized by the Foundation.

3.2 Selection of Investment Funds, Management of Assets. The Foundation does not utilize active asset management. Rather, the pre-approved investment funds will be actively managed by their company’s fund managers.

3.3 Administration. Decisions with respect to the retention, investment, or reinvestment of assets and with respect to commingling of assets shall be made by the Board, or a committee, agent or director authorized by the Board, in accordance with regular procedures. All Funds may be charged an appropriate allocation of direct and indirect expenses attributable to the creation and maintenance of such funds as determined by the Board from time to time.

3.4 Additions/Charges to Fund. The Fund will be allocated a pro-rata portion of the interest and unrealized gains earned in the pooled investment account. The Foundation may charge to the Fund expenses and fees which it customarily charges to other Advised Funds. This annual fee is currently 0%, with no minimum annual charge. Charges are subject to change by resolution of the Board of Foundation. The Fund will also be charged its pro-rata share of the pooled investment account money management fees.

Section 4. General Administrative Provisions

4.1 Donor Advisory Recommendations. The charities selected by donors for the mini-endowments are considered advisory recommendations for receipt of charitable distributions made from the Fund. These recommendations will be considered as existing in perpetuity unless and until changed by the originating donor.

4.2 Balances, Statements and Tax Reporting

Donors have access to a account information including balances, investments and distributions via the Foundation software suite. Donors may also access donation confirmation documents for tax reporting purposes via the Foundation software suite.

4.3 Charity Distribution Eligibility

The Foundation will only distribute grants to any eligible 501(c)(3) public charity in the United States. Charity eligibility is defined by the IRS. Prior to any distribution, the Foundation will verify eligibility via “Deduction Code” and “Foundation Code” for registered charities in the data files available at http:/www.irs.gov/pub/irs-soi/

The Foundation will not distribute grants to:

  • Supporting organizations under section 509(a)(3) of the Internal Revenue Code
  • Churches and houses of worship that have not been issued a tax-exemption letter by the Treasury Department, unless they operate under an umbrella exemption letter
  • Public safety organizations under section 509(a)(4) of the Internal Revenue Code
  • Foreign organizations
  • For-profit organizations, even if limited to charitable purposes
  • Private non-operating foundations

4.4 Limitations. The following limitations apply to all distributions from Advised Funds:

a. Guidelines: giv2giv only approves grants that are used exclusively in furtherance of charitable purposes. In accordance with that policy, giv2giv reserves the right to perform additional due diligence and to decline to make a recommended grant to a charitable organization, including, without limitation, (i) where the grant will confer a more than incidental benefit on an Donor, other person with grant recommendation privileges, or other third party; (ii) where the grant will be used for lobbying, for political contributions, or to support political campaign activities; (iii) where the grant will be used for improper purposes; (iv) where the Donor and related persons control the organization; (v) where giv2giv provides a substantial portion of the organization’s public support; and (vi) for other reasons in accordance with giv2giv policies. Remedial actions may include but are not limited to requiring that the grant be returned or that the Donor make an additional nondeductible contribution.

b. Frequency of Distributions: Foundation will strive to distribute on a quarterly basis. The Board is authorized to approve additional Fund distributions.

c. Stagnant grants: Grants scheduled/queued but not accepted by a charity within 60 days will be subsumed into the giv2giv general operating fund.

d. More than incidental benefit and financial obligations: Grants may not be used to provide a more than incidental benefit to a Donor or other third party. These include school tuition, scholarships sent directly to individuals, certain dues and membership fees, all or a portion of the cost to attend a charitable or other event, goods bought at charitable auctions, raffle tickets, or certain other goods or services. In addition, grants may not be used to satisfy a financial obligation of any individual or entity (including an enforceable pledge).

e. Minimum Distribution: The Foundation strives to make a minimum yearly distribution across all assets of at least 5%. Due to the grant advisory and approval process, in any year distributions may be more or less than 5% of total assets. All distributions will follow the Foundation’s grant advisory and approval process.

4.7 Requirement of Current Distributions. It is the general intent of the Foundation that an amount at least equal to 1.25% of the net assets of the Fund shall be distributed on an ongoing quarterly basis, unless the Board determines that it is in the best interest of the Foundation to accumulate net income, in whole, or in part, for a specific project, which it is contemplated will be accomplished in a reasonable period of time.

Section 5. Reports

5.1 Annual Reports. A report of all distributions from Advised Funds shall be included in the annual report of the Foundation. Additional reports of Advised Funds may be made as prescribed by the Board.

Section 6. Donor Account Inactivity, Deactivation, and Successor Advisors

6.1 Successor Advisors. The Foundation does not recognize successor advisors. However, at point of donor account deactivation or after having passed the defined threshold of inactivity, the assets remaining in the donor’s account will continue to operate through the same advisement/approval/grant procedure as all other assets in the giv2giv.org fund.

6.2 Inactivity. A donor’s account will be considered past the threshold of inactivity if there are no donations into the donor’s account for a period of one year.
